Chord Progression Visualizer

by John Quinlan

Sliders:

  1. Progression length slider (upper left): use the slider to set the length of your chord progression between 3 and 10, inclusive.
  2. Starting chord: Use the slider to toggle between any of the seven offered chords.
  3. Ending chord: Similarly to the starting chord slider-- the most nice-sounding progressions often end with either I or vi, but feel free to try whatever you like!
  4. Key: Use the key slider to transpose into any of the 12 major keys.

Visualization Options:

  1. Bubble visualizer: Simply one circle corresponding to each chord, where each circle lights up when its chord is being played.
  2. Hexagon visualizer: Like the arrangement of keys on an accordion, this option is intuitive in that triads appear as clusters.
  3. Piano visualizer: Visualizes the notes being played as they would appear on a piano.

Chord vs. Time tracer

  1. This visualiztion plots the chords being played as a function of time. Try generating different progressions to find connections between what you hear and what you see.
